# Privacy Policy — Kinematic App

> **DRAFT for legal review.** Effective date: 13 July 2026. Version: 0.1-draft.
> Controller: **Kaiyo Technology Labs**, F-2587, 4th Floor, Ansal Esencia, Sector 67, Gurugram, Haryana 122101, India.

This policy explains how Kaiyo Technology Labs ("we", "us") collects, uses,
shares, and protects personal data through the Kinematic CRM / field-force
platform (the "Service"), and the rights you have. It is written to satisfy the
EU **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and India's **Digital Personal
Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP)**.

## 1. Who this policy covers
- **Customers / prospects ("leads" and "contacts")** whose details are recorded
  in the CRM by our business customers' staff.
- **Field staff / employees** of our business customers who use the mobile apps
  (attendance, location tracking, activity capture).
- **Website / dashboard users.**

For CRM lead/contact data, our business customer is typically the **data
controller / data fiduciary** and we act as a **processor / data processor** on
their instructions (see the Data Processing Agreement). For our own account and
website data we are the controller.

## 2. Personal data we process
| Category | Examples | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Identity & contact | Name, phone/mobile, email, company, job title | Entered by staff, imports, web forms, business-card scan |
| Demographic | Date of birth, gender | Entered by staff (only where the customer enables these fields) |
| Location | Address, city/state/country, postal code; **precise GPS** of field staff during working hours | Forms, device location |
| Images | **Attendance selfies**, form photos | Mobile camera |
| Commercial | Deals, activities, notes, call recordings & transcripts, engagement history | Generated in use |
| Technical | Device model, OS, IP address, app/usage logs | Automatic |

We **minimise** collection: administrators can hide built-in fields (e.g. date
of birth, gender), and hidden fields are not stored server-side.

## 3. Purposes and lawful basis
| Purpose | GDPR lawful basis | DPDP basis |
|---|---|---|
| Provide the CRM/field-force Service to our customer | Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)); processor acting on controller instructions (Art. 28) | Consent given to the data fiduciary / legitimate use (§4) |
| Employee attendance, GPS tracking & selfies | Legitimate interests / employment context (Art. 6(1)(f)/88) — **supported by a DPIA**, not employee "consent" | Employment / notified purpose |
| AI features (lead scoring, summaries, card scan, call intelligence) | Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) with opt-out | Notified purpose |
| Security, fraud/geo-fence checks, audit logging | Legal obligation / legitimate interests | Notified purpose |
| Product analytics & improvement | Legitimate interests | Notified purpose |

Where we rely on **consent**, you may withdraw it at any time (Section 8).

## 4. Special note on employee monitoring
GPS location and attendance selfies are collected **only during working hours /
on-shift** for workforce-management purposes. Because employee consent is not
considered freely given, this processing relies on a documented legitimate
interest and a **Data Protection Impact Assessment**. Staff are notified in-app
before tracking begins.

## 5. Children's data
The Service is **not intended for individuals under 18**. Where a date of birth
indicates a data principal is a minor, DPDP §9 protections apply: we do not
undertake tracking, behavioural monitoring, or targeted advertising of children,
and processing requires verifiable parental/guardian consent. Administrators can
hide the date-of-birth field entirely; where it is collected and indicates a
minor, staff are instructed not to proceed without documented parental/guardian
consent, and the account may be flagged for review.

## 6. Who we share data with
We do not sell personal data. We share it with **sub-processors** strictly to
run the Service (hosting, notifications, AI, tax/e-invoice verification). The
current list, including their locations, is maintained at `SUBPROCESSORS.md` /
https://kinematicapp.com/privacy. Each is bound by a data-processing agreement.

## 7. International transfers
The Service is currently hosted in **Australia — Supabase ap-southeast-2
(Sydney)**, and some sub-processors are located in the **United States**.
Where we transfer personal data across borders we rely on
Standard Contractual Clauses (confirm with counsel), and for Indian data
we comply with DPDP §16. A copy of the safeguards is available on request.

## 8. Your rights
Subject to applicable law, you may: **access** a copy of your data; request
**correction**; request **erasure**; request a **portable** copy; **object to**
or **restrict** processing; and **withdraw consent**. Indian data principals
also have the right to **nominate** and to **grievance redressal**.

To exercise any right, contact **Sagar Bhargava, s@kinematicapp.com**. We
respond within **30 days** and verify your identity first.
Requests are actioned through our data-subject-request tooling (access and
erasure) and recorded in our audit log.

## 9. Retention
We keep personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes above and then
delete or anonymise it per our retention schedule (indicative):
- Active CRM records: for the life of the customer relationship.
- Soft-deleted records: purged **90 days** after deletion.
- GPS / location history: **180 days**.
- Audit/accountability logs: **365 days** (or as legally required).
- Call recordings & transcripts: **180 days**, subject to consent.
Final periods are set in the Data Processing Agreement with each customer.

## 10. Security
We protect personal data with encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+), access
controls, tenant isolation, rate limiting, immutable audit logging, and least-
privilege storage. See our security posture summary for details. No system is
perfectly secure; we operate a breach-response process (Section 11).

## 11. Breach notification
On a personal-data breach we will, without undue delay: notify the relevant
supervisory authority within **72 hours** (GDPR Art. 33) and/or the **Data
Protection Board of India** and affected data principals (DPDP §8(6)), and the
controller where we act as processor.
